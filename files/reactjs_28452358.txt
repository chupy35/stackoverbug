What is the meaning of {...this.props} in Reactjs
<p>What is the meaning of </p>&#xA;&#xA;<pre><code>{...this.props}&#xA;</code></pre>&#xA;&#xA;<p>I am trying to use it like that</p>&#xA;&#xA;<pre><code> &lt;div {...this.props}&gt; Content Here &lt;/div&gt;&#xA;</code></pre>&#xA;
<p>It's called <a href="http://facebook.github.io/react/docs/jsx-spread.html" rel="noreferrer">spread attributes</a> and its aim is to make the passing of props easier.</p>&#xA;&#xA;<p>Let us imagine that you have a component that accepts N number of properties. Passing these down can be tedious and unwieldy if the number grows.</p>&#xA;&#xA;<pre><code>&lt;Component x={} y={} z={} /&gt;&#xA;</code></pre>&#xA;&#xA;<p>Thus instead you do this, wrap them up in an object and use the spread notation</p>&#xA;&#xA;<pre><code>var props = { x: 1, y: 1, z:1 };&#xA;&lt;Component {...props} /&gt;&#xA;</code></pre>&#xA;&#xA;<p>which will unpack it into the props on your component, i.e., you "never" use <code>{... props}</code> inside your <code>render()</code> function, only when you pass the props down to another component. Use your unpacked props as normal <code>this.props.x</code>.</p>&#xA;
<p>When we use our defined components, we can add attributes called props. These attributes are available in our component as this.props and can be used in our render method to render dynamic data:</p>&#xA;&#xA;<pre><code>var MyComponent = React.createClass({&#xA;    render: function(){&#xA;        return (&#xA;            &lt;h1&gt;Hello, {this.props.name}!&lt;/h1&gt;&#xA;        );&#xA;    }&#xA;});&#xA;&#xA;React.render(&lt;MyComponent name="Handsome" /&gt;, document.getElementById('myDiv'));&#xA;</code></pre>&#xA;&#xA;<p>also you can checkout here <a href="https://www.eduonix.com/courses/Web-Development/Getting-Started-With-React-JS-Tutorial-Online" rel="nofollow">reactjs</a> </p>&#xA;
<p>It is ES-6 feature. It means you extract all the properties of props in &#xA;<code>div.{... }</code></p>&#xA;&#xA;<p>operator is used to extract properties of an object.</p>&#xA;
